🌊 Study Notes: The Water Cycle

KPI

Students will describe the cycling of water through Earth's systems driven by energy from the sun and the force of gravity.

🔑 What Drives the Water Cycle?

  • Two driving forces: Energy from the Sun and the force of Gravity
  • The Sun provides energy for evaporation and transpiration
  • Gravity pulls water downhill (runoff) and causes precipitation to fall

🔄 Key Processes

ProcessDefinition
EvaporationA liquid (water) changes into a gas (water vapor) at the surface
TranspirationPlants release water vapor into the atmosphere through leaves
CondensationA gas (water vapor) changes back into a liquid; forms cloud droplets
CrystallizationA liquid turns into a crystalline solid
PrecipitationWater droplets fall as rain, snow, sleet, or hail
Surface RunoffWater flows downhill over the land surface

💧 Where Is Water Stored?

  • On Earth's surface — oceans, lakes, rivers, streams
  • In the atmosphere — as water vapor and in clouds
  • Underground — in cracks and pores (groundwater)
  • Ice caps and glaciers — frozen water at the poles

💡 Storage areas are called RESERVOIRS.

🌍 Earth's Systems (Spheres)

SphereRole
AtmosphereWhere water vapor goes; where clouds form
HydrosphereAll water on Earth (oceans, lakes, rivers, groundwater, ice)
BiosphereLiving things; plants release water via transpiration
GeosphereLand surfaces and underground rocks/soil
  • Atmosphere and hydrosphere overlap (water exists in both)
  • Water leaves biosphere → enters atmosphere through transpiration
  • When water evaporates, it enters the atmosphere

📌 Important Facts

  • The water cycle has NO beginning or end — it is continuous
  • Evaporation happens from bodies of water, trees, humans — all of the above
  • The Sun breaks bonds between water molecules → they escape as gas
  • Clouds form when water vapor rises, cools, and condenses
  • Erosion is NOT a type of precipitation
  • Nitrogen fixation is NOT part of the water cycle
  • Surface runoff flows downhill due to gravity

🧠 Memory Tips

"E-T-C-C-P-R" — Evaporation → Transpiration → Condensation → Cloud formation → Precipitation → Runoff

☀️ Sun = Energy UP (evaporation, transpiration)

⬇️ Gravity = Water DOWN (precipitation, runoff)

🌿 Transpiration = Trans + plant → water transfers out of plants

💧 Condensation = water contracts from gas back to liquid (cooling)

🏺 Reservoirs = Reserve = where water is reserved/stored
